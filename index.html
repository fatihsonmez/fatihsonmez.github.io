Tim Berners-Lee

1) Who is Tim Berners-Lee ?

Sir Timothy John "Tim" Berners-Lee was born on 8 June 1955, London.[i] His parents's names were Mary Lee Woods and Conway Berners Lee. He started to Sheen Mount Primary School. After that, he started to Emanuel School in 1969 and graduated from here in 1973. After graduating from Emanuel School, he started to Queen's College of the University of Oxford in 1973. He graduated from here in 1976 with first-class degree in physics.


2) What Did Tim Berners-Lee Do in His Career Life ?

After graduating from university, he worked at Plessey to create a software for printers. After that, he worked at CERN for June to December 1980, as an independent contractor. He worked on Hypertext project which was named as ENQUIRE.[ii] ENQUIRE was forerunner of HTTP. After leaving CERN, he went to John Poole's Image Computer Systems. Here, he worked on "real-time procedure call". Thanks to this study, he gained experience on computer networking. After all that things, he returned to CERN in 1984, as a fellow.

Figure 1: Tim Berners with Web, CERN, 1990s

He created the first Web Browser and World Wide Web.(see Figure 1) This software was an editor and internet server. It was running on NeXTSTEP, which is an OS, and it was the first server of HTTPd. First website became online on 6 August 1991 and it was about how to use web browser and create a site. After that, he went to MIT and founded Worl Wide Web Consortium to develop web.[iii](see Figure 2) In 2001, he became patron of East Dorset Heritage Trust and in 2004 he joined to Computer Science at the School of Electronics and Computer Science to studying on semantic web. Nowadays, he is working at Alliance for Affordable Internet(A4AI) to make internet more affordable. They want to decrease internet access prices, so that whole world can be online.
